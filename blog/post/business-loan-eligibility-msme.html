Getting a business loan can be challenging if you don't know what banks are looking for. In this comprehensive guide, I'll walk you through every eligibility criterion that Indian banks and NBFCs consider when evaluating MSME loan applications.

1. Credit Score (CIBIL) Requirements

Your personal and business credit scores are the first thing lenders check. For MSME loans in India:

  • Excellent (750+): Best interest rates, quick approval, higher loan amounts
  • Good (700-749): Standard approval, competitive rates
  • Fair (650-699): Possible approval with higher interest or collateral
  • Below 650: Difficulty in approval; may require secured loan or co-applicant

2. Business Vintage (Age of Business)

Most banks prefer businesses that have been operating for at least 2-3 years. Here's how different lenders view business vintage:

Business Vintage Bank Preference Typical Loan Options
Less than 1 year Very difficult Secured loans only (LAP, gold loan)
1-2 years Limited options Small ticket loans, NBFCs
2-3 years Preferred Most bank products available
3+ years Highly preferred Best rates, higher eligibility

3. Annual Turnover & GST Returns

Your business turnover directly impacts loan eligibility. Most lenders use a multiple of your annual turnover to determine loan amount:

  • GST-registered businesses: 15-20% of annual turnover as loan amount (unsecured)
  • Non-GST businesses: Limited options, usually 10-15% of bank statement turnover

4. Income Tax Returns (ITR)

Most formal lenders require at least 2-3 years of ITR filings. They look for:

  • Consistent or growing income
  • Proper classification of business income
  • No major discrepancies between ITR and GST returns

5. Bank Statement Analysis

Lenders analyze your bank statements for:

  • Average monthly balance: Higher balance indicates better cash flow
  • Cash deposits: High cash deposits may raise red flags
  • EMI bounce history: Any bounced cheques or failed payments
  • Regular income credits: Consistent business income
